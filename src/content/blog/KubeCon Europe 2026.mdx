## 🚀 Talk: Lessons Learned Orchestrating Multi-Tenant GPUs on OpenShift AI

### Abstract

Running GPU workloads in a multi-tenant Kubernetes environment presents unique challenges — from resource isolation and scheduling to maximizing hardware utilization. In this session, I'll dive deep into our journey deploying and managing **NVIDIA KAI** and **GH200** GPUs on **Red Hat OpenShift AI**.

### What You'll Learn

- **GPU Orchestration Patterns**: Best practices for scheduling GPU workloads in Kubernetes
- **Multi-Tenancy Challenges**: How to safely share expensive GPU resources across teams
- **NVIDIA KAI Integration**: Leveraging NVIDIA's Kubernetes AI tooling for optimal performance
- **GH200 Grace Hopper**: Real-world experiences with NVIDIA's latest superchip architecture
- **OpenShift AI Features**: Platform capabilities that simplify ML operations at scale
- **Lessons from Production**: What worked, what didn't, and what we'd do differently

---

## 🎯 Why This Matters

GPU compute is expensive. Really expensive. Organizations running AI/ML workloads can't afford to have GPUs sitting idle, but they also need to maintain security boundaries between teams and projects.

This talk addresses the **real operational challenges** of:

- 🔐 **Security**: Isolating workloads without sacrificing performance
- 📊 **Efficiency**: Maximizing GPU utilization across multiple tenants
- ⚡ **Performance**: Reducing scheduling latency for time-sensitive inference workloads
- 🛠️ **Operations**: Managing GPU nodes at scale with GitOps and automation
