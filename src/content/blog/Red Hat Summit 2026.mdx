## 🚀 Talk: GPUs Take Flight — Safety-First Multi-Tenant Platform Engineering with NVIDIA and Red Hat OpenShift AI

### Abstract

As organisations race to deploy AI/ML workloads, GPU infrastructure is becoming the most expensive and contested resource in enterprise IT. This lightning talk distils real-world lessons from building a **safety-first, multi-tenant GPU platform** using **NVIDIA hardware** and **Red Hat OpenShift AI**.

### What You'll Learn

- **Multi-Tenant GPU Isolation**: How to share GPU resources across teams safely, without noisy-neighbour problems
- **Platform Engineering Patterns**: Building self-service GPU platforms that data scientists actually want to use
- **NVIDIA + OpenShift AI Integration**: Practical guidance on combining NVIDIA GPUs with Red Hat's AI/ML platform
- **Safety-First Architecture**: Guardrails, quotas, and policies that prevent runaway workloads from burning through your GPU budget
- **Production Lessons**: Battle-tested patterns and pitfalls from running multi-tenant GPU workloads at scale

---

## 🎯 Why This Talk Matters

GPU compute is the engine behind the AI revolution — but sharing it safely across multiple teams and workloads remains one of the hardest unsolved challenges in platform engineering.

### For Platform Engineers
- **Build GPU platforms with confidence**: Learn isolation patterns that go beyond basic namespace quotas
- **Self-service done right**: Enable data science teams to provision GPU workloads without compromising cluster stability
- **Day-2 operations**: Observability, chargeback, and lifecycle management for GPU infrastructure

### For AI/ML Teams
- **Maximise GPU utilisation**: Move from single-tenant waste to efficient multi-tenant sharing
- **Faster time to production**: Platform engineering patterns that reduce the gap between experimentation and deployment
- **Cost transparency**: Understand exactly what your GPU workloads cost and how to optimise spend

### For Decision Makers
- **Enterprise-grade GPU strategy**: How Red Hat OpenShift AI and NVIDIA create a production-ready foundation
- **Risk mitigation**: Safety-first architecture patterns that protect your investment and your SLAs

> This isn't a vendor pitch — it's an experience report from the trenches of GPU platform engineering. Every recommendation comes from real production workloads.
