Two chances to grab a signed copy, talk Kubernetes, and connect with the cloud-native community.

## Signal Overflow: Observability in the Age of AI

**Monday, March 23 — 18:30 – 21:00**

📍 **Booking.com HQ**, Oosterdokskade 163, Amsterdam

Hosted by **SRE NL** (Site Reliability Engineering Netherlands), this evening event combines talks on observability at scale with networking, food, and a **Kubernetes Recipes book signing**.

**What is on the agenda:**

- Talks on [OpenTelemetry](/blog/opentelemetry-kubernetes-observability-2026/) and observability in the AI-native age
- Networking with SRE practitioners and KubeCon attendees
- Food and drinks at Booking.com's iconic Amsterdam headquarters
- **Free Kubernetes Recipes copies** — signed by the author

This is the perfect way to kick off KubeCon week. The SRE NL community brings together engineers who operate real production systems, and the conversations are always practical and hands-on.

**RSVP required — [Register on Luma](https://lu.ma/o93w1hxi)**

Bring a valid ID for building access.

## Book Signing at vCluster Booth #521

**Tuesday, March 24 — 12:30 – 13:30**

📍 **vCluster Booth #521**, KubeCon Europe 2026, RAI Amsterdam

Stop by the [vCluster](https://www.vcluster.com/) booth during the Day 1 lunch break for a **Kubernetes Recipes book signing**. Grab a free signed copy and chat about virtual clusters, [multi-tenancy](/blog/kubernetes-multi-tenant-gpu-isolation/), and [platform engineering](/blog/kubernetes-recipes-mindset-cto-platform-strategy/).

vCluster is one of the most interesting projects in the Kubernetes ecosystem — virtual clusters that give teams isolated Kubernetes environments without the cost of separate physical clusters. If you are building an [internal developer platform](/blog/devex-internal-developer-platform/), this is a conversation worth having.

## About Kubernetes Recipes

[Kubernetes Recipes](https://kubernetes.recipes/) is a practical guide to solving real-world Kubernetes challenges. Every chapter gives you a reusable pattern you can apply immediately — from deployment strategies to [GPU orchestration](/blog/nvidia-gpu-operator-kubernetes-mig-guide/), from [security](/blog/zero-trust-kubernetes-security-2026/) to [observability](/blog/opentelemetry-kubernetes-observability-2026/).

The [recipe mindset](/blog/kubernetes-recipes-mindset-cto-platform-strategy/) is what makes it different: practical solutions over abstract theory. Whether you are a developer deploying workloads or a CTO evaluating [platform strategy and ROI](/blog/kubernetes-transform-unlock-roi-business-value/), the book meets you where you are.
