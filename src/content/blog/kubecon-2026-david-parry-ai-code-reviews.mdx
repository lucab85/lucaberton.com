## The code review problem nobody talks about

Pull requests pile up. Maintainers burn out. Subtle bugs slip through because the reviewer was on their fifth PR of the day and their eyes glazed over at line 247. Tests and linters catch the obvious stuff — but the 3-AM-wake-up-call bugs? Those need a second set of eyes that never gets tired.

**David Parry** thinks AI is that second set of eyes.

## Who is David Parry

David is a **Principal Architect at [Qodo](https://qodo.ai)** with decades of experience in enterprise architecture, Java, and building scalable systems. He is not a newcomer chasing the AI hype — he is a seasoned architect who has seen enough production incidents to know that code quality is not a nice-to-have, it is the difference between sleeping through the night and getting paged at 3 AM.

His focus: **agent-driven developer productivity**. Not AI that generates code for you to debug, but AI that reviews code alongside you — catching the patterns, edge cases, and architectural concerns that humans miss when they are context-switching between twelve repositories.

**If you maintain an open-source project, Qodo will sponsor your code reviews — for free.**

For open-source maintainers who are already volunteering their time, this removes one of the biggest bottlenecks: the review queue. Head to [qodo.ai](https://qodo.ai) to get started.

## Agent MCPs: bridging AI and developer workflows

David is speaking at **KubeAutoDay** on March 23rd at the Amstel Boathouse in Amsterdam. His session, **"Introduction to the Agent MCPs"**, dives into how **Model Context Protocols** are becoming the bridge between AI agents, developer workflows, and system tools.

MCPs matter because they solve the integration problem. Having a smart AI agent is useless if it cannot access your codebase, understand your CI pipeline, read your Kubernetes manifests, or interact with your issue tracker. MCPs provide the standardized protocol layer that connects AI agents to the tools developers already use — without building custom integrations for every combination.

This is directly relevant to anyone building [AI-powered developer platforms](/blog/backstage-plugins-internal-developer-portal/) or thinking about how agentic AI fits into their engineering workflow.
