I am proud to share that I have officially earned the **KubeCon + CloudNativeCon Europe 2026 Speaker** badge, issued by **The Linux Foundation** on April 3, 2026.

![KubeCon + CloudNativeCon Europe 2026 Speaker Badge](/blog/events/kubecon-2026-speaker-badge.png)

## What This Badge Represents

According to the credential description:

> Earners of this badge are speakers, selected by the KubeCon + CloudNativeCon Europe 2026 Co-Chairs, who have provided thought-leadership and shared best practices with attendees during their talk(s). They are Open Source and Cloud Native Technology Subject Matter Experts on the topic(s) they presented.

This is not a badge you can purchase or self-certify. You earn it by being **selected through the CFP (Call for Proposals) process** and delivering your talk at the conference.

## My KubeCon EU 2026 Talk

I presented **"Multi-Tenant GPUs on Bare Metal Kubernetes"** — a session that drew a [packed room](/blog/kubecon-2026-talk-multi-tenant-gpus-recap/) of attendees eager to learn how to share expensive GPU resources across teams without the overhead of virtualization.

The talk covered:
- GPU partitioning strategies (MIG, time-slicing, MPS) on bare metal
- Kubernetes device plugins and dynamic resource allocation
- Real-world multi-tenant scheduling patterns
- Cost optimization for [AI inference workloads](/blog/inference-gold-rush-ai-startups-kubernetes-2026/)

You can [download the slides (PDF)](/slides/kubecon-europe-2026-multi-tenant-gpus-bare-metal.pdf) from the [KubeCon landing page](/kubecon/).
