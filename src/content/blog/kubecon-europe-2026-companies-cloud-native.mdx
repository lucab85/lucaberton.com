KubeCon EU 2026 in Amsterdam is not just 15,000 engineers — it is also the companies building the tools, platforms, and infrastructure the ecosystem depends on. Here are the organizations making an impact this week.

---

## iits-consulting — GitOps and Kubara

*[Read the full profile →](/blog/iits-consulting-kubecon-eu-2026-gitops-kubara/)*

**[iits-consulting](https://iits-consulting.de)** is launching **[Kubara](https://kubara.io)** at KubeCon — an open-source Kubernetes platform framework built entirely on GitOps. Bootstrap a production-ready platform in under 30 minutes with ArgoCD, External Secrets Operator, and Kyverno.

Led by [Artem Lajko](/blog/kubecon-2026-artem-lajko-gitops-kubara/), Head of Platform Engineering and co-author of *"Implementing GitOps with Kubernetes"*, the team is at **Booth B495** with book signings, a Steam Deck giveaway, and deep GitOps conversations.

**Why they matter:** They build in the open — open-source framework, free GitOps course, published book. The best demonstration of expertise is giving it away.

---

## Redpill Linpro — Sovereign Nordic Cloud

*[Read the full profile →](/blog/redpill-linpro-kubecon-eu-2026-sovereign-cloud/)*

**[Redpill Linpro](https://redpill-linpro.com)** is building the **Nordic Cloud** — sovereign, GDPR-compliant infrastructure operating from three data centers in the Oslo region, powered by 100% renewable energy.

With [Pepijn Oomen](/blog/kubecon-2026-pepijn-oomen-sovereign-cloud/) leading their container technology strategy, Redpill Linpro offers a genuine European alternative to American hyperscalers. No CLOUD Act exposure. No sovereignty theater.

**Why they matter:** When 90% of Norwegian municipalities run email on US providers, the need for truly sovereign infrastructure is not theoretical — it is urgent.

---

## Qodo — AI-Powered Code Reviews

*[Read the full profile →](/blog/qodo-kubecon-eu-2026-ai-code-reviews/)*

**[Qodo](https://qodo.ai)** is bringing AI agents to code review — and making it **free for open-source projects**. Their platform understands your entire codebase context, not just the diff, providing feedback a senior engineer would give.

[David Parry](/blog/kubecon-2026-david-parry-ai-code-reviews/), Principal Architect, is speaking at **KubeAutoDay** (March 23rd) on Agent MCPs — the protocol layer connecting AI agents to developer workflows.

**Why they matter:** Open-source maintainers are drowning in review queues. Qodo removes the bottleneck — every PR reviewed, every time.

## Why company profiles matter

KubeCon is not just about Kubernetes. It is about the ecosystem of companies — from startups to established players — that build the tools, run the infrastructure, and support the community.

The companies profiled here share common traits:

- **Open-source first** — they contribute to the ecosystem, not just extract from it
- **Community-driven** — sponsoring events, publishing free courses, donating to Rejekts
- **Solving real problems** — sovereignty, code quality, platform sprawl
- **People-centered** — led by engineers who care about the humans using the technology

If you are at KubeCon EU 2026 in Amsterdam, seek these companies out. The expo hall conversations are where partnerships start.
