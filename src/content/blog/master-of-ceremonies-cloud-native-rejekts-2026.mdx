## From speaker to Master of Ceremonies

When the [Cloud Native Rejekts](https://cloud-native.rejekts.io/) organizers asked me to be the **Master of Ceremonies** for the EU 2026 edition in Amsterdam, I did not hesitate for a second. Rejekts is the conference I look forward to the most during KubeCon week — raw, technical, zero marketing fluff, and packed with the kind of people who build the cloud native ecosystem from the ground up.

Co-hosting alongside the brilliant **Julia Hahn**, we welcomed **over 300 engineers** into the Miro office for a full day of the deepest technical content you will find anywhere outside the main KubeCon venue.

## What is Cloud Native Rejekts

If you have never heard of it, [Cloud Native Rejekts](https://cloud-native.rejekts.io/) is the ultimate **B-side conference**. It welcomes talks that were rejected from KubeCon + CloudNativeCon — not because they were bad, but because KubeCon receives thousands of submissions and only accepts a fraction.

The result is a curated lineup of incredibly strong proposals covering:

- **Core Kubernetes concepts** and deep internals
- **Open source tooling** — the projects you will be running in production next year
- **User experience stories** — real-world failures and successes, no vendor pitch
- **Security** — supply chain, runtime, policy engines
- **New experiments** — the bleeding edge that has not made it to CNCF yet

The format includes **30-minute full-length talks** and **5-minute lightning talks**, creating a fast-paced rhythm that keeps the energy high all day.

## The MC role — what it actually involves

Being an MC is fundamentally different from being a speaker. When you give a talk, you prepare one story and tell it well. When you MC an entire conference, you become the **connective tissue** between every session.

Here is what the role looked like in practice:

### Before the event

- **Study every single talk abstract** — you cannot introduce a speaker properly if you do not understand their topic
- **Coordinate with organizers** on schedule changes, last-minute speaker swaps, and room logistics
- **Prepare transitions** — how do you bridge a deep eBPF networking talk to a platform engineering case study without whiplash?
- **Rehearse timing** — Rejekts runs on a tight schedule, and the MC is the clock

### During the event

- **Set the energy from minute one** — the opening sets the tone for the entire day
- **Introduce each speaker** with context that makes the audience lean in
- **Manage Q&A** — keep questions sharp, redirect when needed, make sure quiet audience members get heard
- **Handle the unexpected** — projector issues, schedule shifts, speakers running over time
- **Keep 300 engineers engaged** for 8+ hours — that is a marathon, not a sprint

### After the event

- **Connect people** — the hallway track at Rejekts is where the real magic happens
- **Follow up with speakers** — share feedback, amplify their content on social media
- **Document lessons** for the next edition

## Why Rejekts matters more than you think

At a 15,000-person KubeCon, you are one face in a massive crowd. At Rejekts, you are in a room where every person chose to be there for the technical depth. The conversations are more honest. The feedback is more direct. The connections are more real.

Some of the best talks I have ever seen at KubeCon week were not at KubeCon — they were at Rejekts.

If you are a speaker whose CFP was rejected, **submit to Rejekts**. Your talk deserves an audience, and the Rejekts audience is one of the most engaged in the entire ecosystem.

## Lessons from the stage

After years of [speaking at conferences](/conference-speaking-journey/) and now MC-ing Rejekts, here are the patterns I keep seeing:

1. **Energy is contagious** — if the MC is excited, the room follows
2. **Brevity is respect** — nobody came to hear the MC talk; keep intros short and sharp
3. **Names matter** — pronounce every speaker's name correctly, every time
4. **Read the room** — after a dense technical talk, the audience needs 30 seconds to breathe before the next intro
5. **The hallway is part of the program** — create space for organic conversations between sessions

## Join the next edition

Cloud Native Rejekts runs at every KubeCon — EU and NA. The CFP typically opens in December and closes in January. Follow [@rejabornerds](https://twitter.com/rejabornerds) or check [cloud-native.rejekts.io](https://cloud-native.rejekts.io/) for announcements.

If you are attending KubeCon, block the day before for Rejekts. You will not regret it.
