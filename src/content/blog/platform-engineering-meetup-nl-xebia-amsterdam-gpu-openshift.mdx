## My Talk: Lessons Learned Orchestrating Multi-Tenant GPUs on OpenShift AI

How do you run shared, production-grade GPUs for AI/ML safely and efficiently?

In this experience report, I share lessons learned from implementing **multi-tenant GPU orchestration on OpenShift AI** using **NVIDIA KAI on G/H200 hardware**, fronted by Traefik and backed by Dell Technologies platforms.

### What I Cover

- **Tenant isolation patterns** — namespaces, quotas, and priority classes for safe multi-tenancy
- **Scheduling across heterogeneous nodes** — mixing GPU types without manual node selection
- **MIG versus full-GPU trade-offs** — when to partition, when to dedicate
- **Throughput versus latency tuning** — optimizing for inference SLAs vs training throughput
- **Driver and firmware pitfalls** — the bugs you only find in production
- **Upgrade and rollback strategies** — zero-downtime GPU driver updates
- **Day-2 operations** — observability, autoscaling, and chargeback models

This talk builds directly on my [KubeCon Europe 2026 presentation](/kubecon/) on multi-tenant GPUs and my [Red Hat Summit 2026 lightning talk](/blog/red-hat-summit-2026/) — but goes deeper into the operational lessons that only emerge after months of running GPU workloads in production.

## Why This Matters

Platform engineering is no longer just about CI/CD pipelines and developer portals. The rise of AI workloads has added a new dimension: **GPU orchestration is now a platform engineering problem**. Teams need to share expensive hardware across multiple tenants, enforce quotas, provide self-service access, and deliver observability — all while keeping costs under control.

This meetup sits at exactly that intersection: cloud cost management meets AI platform operations.
