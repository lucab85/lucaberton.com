## The company building Europe's cloud independence

While American hyperscalers dominate the conversation, a Nordic company is quietly building the infrastructure that European organizations actually need: **sovereign, GDPR-compliant, 100% green energy cloud services** — operated entirely under European jurisdiction.

**[Redpill Linpro](https://redpill-linpro.com)** is not a startup chasing the sovereignty buzzword. They are a mature open-source services provider with deep roots in the Nordic IT ecosystem, and they are at KubeCon EU 2026 with a clear message: your data deserves European control.

## What Redpill Linpro brings to KubeCon

### Nordic Cloud

The flagship offering: a cloud platform hosted across **three data centers in the Oslo region**, fully operated within GDPR jurisdiction. No American parent company, no CLOUD Act exposure, no ambiguity.

This is not "hosted in EU" theater. As highlighted in a recent analysis, [90% of Norwegian municipalities run email on American providers](/blog/kubecon-2026-pepijn-oomen-sovereign-cloud/) — 301 out of 357 on Microsoft 365 alone. Redpill Linpro's Nordic Cloud is the alternative for organizations that take sovereignty seriously.

Key capabilities:

- **GDPR-native architecture** — data stays under European legal jurisdiction, full stop
- **100% renewable energy** — powered by Norwegian hydroelectric, one of the cleanest energy grids in the world
- **Kubernetes-native** — standard orchestration, no vendor lock-in, portable workloads
- **Open Source foundation** — transparency, auditability, community-driven security
- **Multi-site high availability** — three data centers for redundancy and disaster recovery

### Professional open source services

Beyond cloud hosting, Redpill Linpro provides managed services, consulting, and support for the full open-source stack:

- **Kubernetes platform engineering** — design, deployment, and operations
- **Container technology** — migration, optimization, security hardening
- **Linux infrastructure** — the foundation they have been building on for decades
- **Security and compliance** — GDPR, NIS2, and industry-specific requirements

## Why it matters for your organization

The [digital sovereignty](/blog/sovereign-cloud-eu-data-residency-infrastructure/) conversation has shifted from "nice to have" to "board-level priority":

- **GDPR enforcement is getting stricter** — regulators want to see genuine compliance, not checkbox exercises
- **The EU AI Act adds data residency requirements** — AI workloads processing European data need European infrastructure
- **US-EU data transfer frameworks remain legally fragile** — Schrems III is not a question of if, but when
- **Public sector mandates** are increasingly requiring European-only infrastructure
- **The [EU Cyber Resilience Act](/blog/cra-cicd-compliance-automation/)** adds supply chain security requirements

Redpill Linpro is not reacting to these trends — they have been building sovereign infrastructure for years. When the regulations catch up, their customers are already compliant.
