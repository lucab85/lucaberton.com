Great energy at the **Superteam NL Launch Event** on the Solana boat in Amsterdam — the Supperclub Cruise on the IJ waterfront.

Always exciting to see the Dutch ecosystem showing up with builders, operators, and crypto-native talent all in one place.

## The Event

[Superteam NL](https://luma.com/q7cngmfs) is the Netherlands chapter of Superteam, the global Solana community. The launch event brought together the Dutch Web3 ecosystem for an evening of networking, conversations, and community building — literally on a boat cruising through Amsterdam's harbor.

Big thanks to SuperteamNL for bringing everyone together, and shoutout to CryptoCanal, Wigby/Mick, Collins Ejiofor, and everyone pushing the space forward.

## Why This Matters

From a technology perspective, Web3 infrastructure shares more with cloud-native engineering than most people realize:

- **Distributed systems** — blockchain networks are distributed systems at their core
- **Infrastructure as code** — node deployment and management benefits from [Ansible](/blog/private-ai-cloud-rhel-ai-instructlab/) and [Terraform](/blog/multi-cloud-terraform-patterns/) automation
- **[Kubernetes](/blog/kubernetes-recipes-mindset-cto-platform-strategy/) orchestration** — validator nodes and RPC endpoints run on the same container orchestration platforms as any other workload
- **[Observability](/blog/opentelemetry-kubernetes-observability-2026/)** — monitoring blockchain infrastructure requires the same tooling and practices as traditional cloud

The overlap between Web3 infrastructure and [platform engineering](/blog/kubernetes-transform-unlock-roi-business-value/) is significant. The same skills that make you effective at running [GPU clusters](/blog/nvidia-gpu-operator-kubernetes-setup/) or [cloud infrastructure](/blog/aws-availability-zone-disruption-dubai-cloud-resilience/) apply directly to blockchain node operations.

## Amsterdam's Position

Amsterdam continues to punch above its weight as a tech hub. Between the [AI community](/blog/ai-house-amsterdam-agentic-commerce-wallets/), the cloud-native ecosystem leading up to [KubeCon Europe 2026](/blog/kubecon-europe-2026-side-events/), and now a growing Web3 presence, the city is building density across multiple technology verticals.

As the [Prosus State of AI report](/blog/state-of-ai-europe-prosus-report-2026/) highlighted, European tech hubs need critical mass to compete globally. Events like this contribute to that density.
