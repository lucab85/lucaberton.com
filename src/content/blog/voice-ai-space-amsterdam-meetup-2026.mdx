I attended the **[Voice AI Space Amsterdam Meetup](https://luma.com/voiceaiamsterdam)** — the latest stop in a global tour that has already hit Barcelona, London, Dubai, Paris, and NYC. The Voice AI community is growing fast, and Amsterdam was the natural next stop.

![Voice AI Space presenter on stage — voiceaispace.com central hub for everything Voice AI](/blog/events/voice-ai-amsterdam-1.jpg)

The event was hosted at a venue with impressive production quality — blue-lit stage, professional setup, and a packed room of voice AI builders, researchers, and entrepreneurs.

## The Format

The meetup followed a proven formula:
- **5:00 PM** — Doors open, networking
- **5:45 PM** — Demos and talks from builders
- **7:00 PM** — Food, drinks, and more networking

## The Talks

![Full-body shot of Voice AI Space organizer presenting the platform vision](/blog/events/voice-ai-amsterdam-2.jpg)

### Matt Coler — Sarcasm Detection in Voice AI (University of Groningen)

Matt leads a research team working on one of voice AI's hardest open problems: getting systems to understand not just **what** people say, but **what they mean**. Sarcasm is a particularly useful lens for probing the limits of pragmatic inference — combining speech, text, and vision to catch what any single channel misses. This is fundamental research that will define how natural future voice assistants feel.

### Thomas Kluiters — Reson8 Speech Recognition

Thomas Kluiters, Head of AI at **Reson8** (a local Amsterdam voice AI company), presented their vision for Voice AI and demonstrated their unique speech recognition capabilities. Local champions building competitive speech-to-text is exactly what the European AI ecosystem needs.

### Ahmed Mamdouh Khalil — The Rise and Fall of ASCENSCIA

A refreshingly honest post-mortem: Ahmed shared lessons learned from building an AI Voice Assistant for scientific labs. The "rise and fall" framing is rare in tech meetups — most people only share success stories. Understanding why voice AI products fail in specialized domains is just as valuable as knowing what works.

### Kevin Litwiak — Live Vibe-Coding a Voice AI Agent with Vapi

Kevin live-coded a Voice AI agent using Claude and [Vapi](https://vapi.ai/) capabilities — building in real-time on stage. He also shared inspiring stories from other Voice AI builders in the community. Vapi is emerging as a leading platform for building voice-first AI agents with minimal infrastructure.

### Phanos Anastasiou — ChickyTutor Demo

Phanos demoed **ChickyTutor**, an AI language tutor designed for everyone. Voice-first language learning is a natural fit — pronunciation feedback, conversational practice, and real-time correction all benefit enormously from speech AI.

### Deepak Singla — Fini Multi-Modal AI Agent

Deepak demonstrated the voice capabilities of **Fini**, a multi-modal AI agent built for enterprise fintech support. The financial services sector is one of the highest-value applications for voice AI — handling customer queries, compliance verification, and transaction support through natural conversation.

## Voice AI Space: The Central Hub

![Close-up of the Voice AI Space branding — Products, News, Knowledge, Jobs, Events at voiceaispace.com](/blog/events/voice-ai-amsterdam-3.jpg)

[voiceaispace.com](https://voiceaispace.com) positions itself as the **central hub for everything Voice AI** — covering products, news, knowledge, jobs, and events. With meetups now running across 6+ cities globally, they are building real community infrastructure for the voice AI ecosystem.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Voice AI is maturing fast** — from research labs to production deployments in fintech, education, and enterprise support
2. **Sarcasm and pragmatic inference** remain unsolved — understanding intent beyond words is the next frontier
3. **European voice AI is thriving** — local companies like Reson8 building competitive speech recognition
4. **Honest failure stories** create more value than polished success narratives at meetups
5. **Vibe-coding voice agents** is now possible — tools like Vapi + Claude enable rapid prototyping on stage

The voice AI space (pun intended) is one of the fastest-growing niches in AI right now. With multimodal models adding native speech capabilities and latency dropping below human perception thresholds, we are approaching a tipping point where voice-first interfaces become the default for many applications.
